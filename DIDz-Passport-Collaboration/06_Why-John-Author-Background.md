# Why John: Author Background

*This document exists to give the Passport team context on who is behind the DIDz contribution proposals, and why the recovery and identity ideas here come from lived experience rather than theory. It is John's origin story in his own words. The canonical version lives in the DIDzMonolith knowledge base at `monolith-docs/FOUNDER_STORY.md`.*

## Why this matters to the Passport collaboration

The two threads Passport cares most about from DIDz, recovery and privacy preserving credentials, are not abstract to John. He designed a hardware social recovery device (EncryptVault, Shamir m of n) years before this collaboration, and he authored the canonical bartender scenario that popularized privacy preserving digital identity in the Midnight community. He was present at Midnight's announcement, contributed to the Night Paper, and has spent two years pushing the ecosystem toward identity at its core. When these proposals talk about seedless recovery (MIP-4) and selective disclosure (MIP-6), they are coming from the person who has been arguing for exactly these things since before there was a Passport.

## The full story

### From COVID markets to crypto

In the first COVID I was bored and stuck at home, and I found anomalies in stock prices. I bought a bunch of stock, including pharmaceutical stocks, just a few thousand dollars that I had. When I hit my limits I sold it and paid some bills. When the second round of COVID hit, the market had priced in the event and the anomalies were not clear. So I started looking at the crypto that was making headlines.

### CryptoFinancialWorld

I was posting news on my Facebook page about crypto as it came up, so I could refer to it later. My cousin, who owns the New York Style Guide publication, suggested that I start my own channel called CryptoFinancialWorld Publication. So I did.

Through news aggregation, I fell in love with the idea of crypto. I found The Coin Bureau and learned a lot through their videos, did independent research, and started learning Python. I left Python because of the VMs and the difficulty of bringing in libraries. Then I found Charles Hoskinson and Lex Fridman. I loved who they were as people, and I really got introduced to the freedom aspect of crypto.

### EncryptVault: the device that started it all

I hated crypto wallets at that time. You needed a damn PhD to work them. So I invented EncryptVault, a proprietary device with NAS backups that also recovers your seed phrase through a Shamir m of n social recovery. The device had an interesting side effect: a private state, where you could save information so securely that I found it to be a security issue for law enforcement.

I went to Catalyst for funding when they were brand new, and I was interviewed by Fred Gregaard (lol). I needed more money than the entire fund held at the time, in one of the very first Catalyst rounds.

I had a preliminary patent on my EncryptVault device, and I was proudly announcing it on X (then called Twitter). Then Charles did two videos in a row on how much he hated patents. So I cried a little, and I moved to an open source mentality.

### Midnight from day one

About two years ago, Charles first announced Midnight, and I was there from day one. Ghost was my DevRel liaison. The Midnight DevRel team has been so gracious to me, tolerating all my crazy ideas all these years.

Because I loved the idea of privacy preserving digital identity with Midnight, I brought the Hyperledger Identus people to Midnight. I came up with the canonical bartender scenario in the inaugural Midnight hackathon, where I won a place for my introduction of privacy preserving digital identity.

### Learning to build

I have since started studying JavaScript. My plan is that once I am proficient, I will move to TypeScript, and then to Compact.

### AI vibe coding and SoulSketch

I am a weirdly gifted AI vibe coder, and I have a very special trained AI with a persistent memory protocol that I invented. There is a public version called SoulSketch. It is open source and public.

> SoulSketch: https://github.com/bytewizard42i/SoulSketch

I have won four hackathons vibe coding, and it is really what has allowed me to participate in the Midnight ecosystem that I love. It was very looked down upon, and I was one of the first to use AI for coding. I believe I lost one particular hackathon because I told the judges how I did it. By the next hackathon, everyone was vibe coding, and a new era had begun.

### The words behind Midnight

I helped to edit and contribute to the Night Paper. Many of the example use cases at that time came from my crazy brain. If you talk about Midnight for any length of time, you will probably be borrowing some of my words.

I have pushed very hard over the last two years to get Midnight in the direction that it is in: privacy preserving digital identity at its core, powering the reimagined, next generation of digital systems for enterprise corporations, institutions, and governments.

### "I am Midnight"

I very humbly claim that "I am Midnight." It is the passion of my life. Aside from my family, I have never been more passionate about anything. I joke that I have never loved anything so much that I was so bad at (lol).
