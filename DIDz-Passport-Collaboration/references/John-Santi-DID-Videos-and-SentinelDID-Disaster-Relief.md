## SentinelDID — disaster-relief identity

3. **SentinelDID**
   https://youtu.be/27p2M7FLx8E?si=wJlY7nhlwF30jH6c
   A project aimed at **disaster relief**. Motivating scenario: **Venezuela**. The concept
   combines **World Mobile** (connectivity), **Starlink** (backhaul), and **Ai** to
   establish and use identity in crisis conditions where normal infrastructure and devices
   are unavailable.

### Why it belongs in the Passport recovery conversation
The disaster-relief case is the extreme stress test for Passport's P5 (recover-from-zero)
and C14/C15: a person with no devices, no reliable network, and possibly no local social
graph must still re-attach to their soulbound account. It argues for:
- **Infrastructure-light, offline-tolerant** recovery helpers (C15, P8).
- **Soulbound biometric + credential factors** that survive total device loss (file `01`, §3).
- **Substitutable helpers / issuers** so recovery does not depend on any single operator
  that a disaster might take offline.
